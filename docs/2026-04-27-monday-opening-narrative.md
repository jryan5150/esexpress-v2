## The Core Reframe (Lexcom → ES Express)

> "I think that narrative is what we need to start with Monday. Show them, via their own lens, what they've been building too.
>
> Sure it's been frustrating. But let me show you why.
>
> Your team had been building toward a system that's proprietary since 2021. Color-coded states. Discrepancies tracked manually every week. Different flows to facilitate reconciliation. You were specing this system all along — you just had the wrong compiler the first time. Thought it was a typical TMS software project. That's why the friction existed there.
>
> Lexcom: we did exactly what I told you we would do the day you met me. We took the input, the feedback, the evolutionary feedback data from your process — and admittedly with some trouble and frustration — we finally got to where we are today. A system that was able to surface your mental model. Because we had the back-and-forth. Because you guys did exactly what we talked about: molded the system to give to you, instead of us telling you to mold yourself to a system that doesn't understand how you operate."

---

## Why This Frame Works

1. **It validates their frustration without absorbing blame.** The friction is named, not minimized. But it's reframed as _spec discovery_, not _failure_.
2. **It returns ownership to them.** The team's color-coded sheet, the discrepancy column they've maintained since 2022, Jenny's reconciliation tabs, Jodi's payroll workbook — these aren't _workarounds_, they're the _design document_. v2 just compiled it.
3. **It honors the original commitment.** Day one Lexcom promised "give to the user, don't make them mold to the system." Today's v2 surfaces that promise as a working artifact.
4. **It positions the engagement as collaborative discovery, not vendor-customer.** The team co-authored what got built.
5. **It de-risks the SOW conversation.** "We mold to your operation" is now demonstrably true — the unmold-to-software fear is the thing the prior dev couldn't address. We did.

## What NOT To Do With This Frame

- Don't apologize for the timeline. The reframe IS the apology made constructive.
- Don't recap features. Show the system reading their truth, not what we built around it.
- Don't promise more before close on this scope. Hold the SOW gate tight per `project_round_4_is_the_gate`.
- Don't pivot from this script if she pushes back. The script reframes; pushback is data, not failure.
