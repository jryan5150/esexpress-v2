### The cross-naming pattern is now explicit

The calibration earlier tonight revealed v2 and PCS track different views of the same business. This discovery confirms **the historical dispatch data carries BOTH naming conventions:**

- **Liberty-prefixed names** = carrier-side identifier (what PropX/Logistiq feeds v2)
- **Comstock/ATMZ/HV-prefixed names** = customer-side identifier (what PCS billing knows)
- **Both exist as separate rows in daily_dispatch.dest_name over the 3-year corpus**

The cross-reference between them (e.g. which Liberty Apache Baytex X is the same physical well as which Comstock Y) is recoverable from `raw.shipper_consignee_locations` and `raw.location_shipping_activity` which the flywheel hasn't fully exploited yet.

## Caveats

- The 163 "wells detected" used keyword classification (Liberty, Comstock, HV, Apache, etc.) which is heuristic. Some may be origin/loader sites; some real wells will have been missed. Jessica + Jenny's eyes are the arbiter.
- Geographic hints in the table (dest_city) are load-weighted mode, not authoritative.
- The 14 aliases applied tonight used token_set_ratio ≥90% + suffix containment. Acceptable false-positive rate ~low, but one or two may be wrong — visible in data_integrity_runs if they produce unexpected matches.
- `dest_name` in daily_dispatch sometimes carries a sandplant (return trip). Doesn't affect the well candidates we identified but matters for full understanding of the dataset.
